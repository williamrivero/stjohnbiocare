# PSN Dialysis Personnel — Qualifications, Duties & Responsibilities

**Source:** Philippine Society of Nephrology (PSN), *Guidelines for Nephrologists in the
Operation of Hemodialysis Clinics in the Philippines*, **3rd Edition, 2024**, Section A —
Personnel (Medical Personnel §1, Non-Medical Personnel §2).

**Purpose of this file:** the single source of truth for writing **Careers-page job postings**
for St. John Biocare / Core Renal / St. Josef centers. Every role below is transcribed from the
PSN guideline so postings stay compliant and consistent. When drafting a new posting, pull the
qualifications and duties from the matching role here. Do **not** invent requirements beyond
these; if a posting needs more, mark it as a St. John Biocare–specific addition (e.g. "no
derogatory record") separate from the PSN baseline.

Abbreviations: **HDC** = Hemodialysis Clinic/Center · **PRC** = Professional Regulation
Commission · **PSN** = Philippine Society of Nephrology · **DOH** = Department of Health ·
**AN** = Attending Nephrologist · **POD** = Physician on Duty · **BLS/ACLS** = Basic /
Advanced Cardiac Life Support · **AAMI** = Association for the Advancement of Medical
Instrumentation · **RO** = Reverse Osmosis · **QAPI/CQI** = Quality Assessment & Performance
Improvement / Continuous Quality Improvement.

## 1. MEDICAL PERSONNEL

### 1.1 Dialysis Clinical Head (DCH)
*Oversees the medical operations of the unit and ensures all dialysis procedures are within
acceptable norms of practice and regulatory standards. Different from a hospital medical director.*

**Qualifications**
- PRC-licensed physician, and a **nephrologist certified by the Philippine Specialty Boards of
  the PSN** (PSN provides DOH an updated list of board-certified members).
- In practice in the local area, easily accessible to the HDC.
- Holds a **Certificate of Attestation** (for Nephrologist) or Certificate of Attestation for
  No Available Nephrologist (for non-nephrologist) from PSN.
- Holds a **PSN or Local Chapter Endorsement for Dialysis Clinical Head** (in good standing).
- Where no board-certified nephrologist is available, DOH allows, in order of priority:
  (i) Board Eligible in Nephrology; (ii) Board-certified in IM/Pediatrics with ≥6 months in a
  DOH-licensed HDC; (iii) Board-eligible in IM/Pediatrics with ≥6 months; (iv) General
  Practitioner with ≥6 months, in DOH-identified areas of need. Non-nephrologist training must
  follow PSN accreditation guidelines; time-limited handling (non-board-certified nephrologist
  up to 3 years; non-nephrologist up to 1 year, one HDC only) with an owner undertaking to
  replace once a board-certified nephrologist is available.
- Compensation per current PSN guidelines, covered by a **service contract** with the HDC owner.

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Involved in HDC design/planning before the Permit to Construct.
- Oversees overall patient care, hemodialysis access protocols, dialysis solutions/safe water,
  safe & functional equipment, and operator skill/competence.
- Provides owners the DOH medical-operations requirements; develops medical protocols, policies
  and procedures per DOH/PSN standards.
- Sets education/performance criteria; assesses credentials of medical employees before hire.
- Identifies training gaps; initiates staff training and certification.
- Initiates/implements **QAPI**; conducts documented **CQI meetings at least monthly**.
- Requires compliance with infection control & surveillance; recommends AAMI-based water quality
  standards (safe mixing, monitoring, complete water treatment/distribution).
- **Visits the HDC ≥4× a month (≈weekly)** (GIDA exception allowed); virtual visit during
  disasters/pandemics, documented.
- Handles a **maximum of 3 HDCs** (up to 5 in nephrologist-scarce/GIDA areas).
- Allows patients to choose their own qualified Attending Nephrologist; if a co-owner, must not
  be the exclusive AN for all patients.

*Not responsible for:* day-to-day operations (that is the Unit Operations Manager's job),
daily equipment/supply checks, other staff's professional negligence, or acting as employer.

### 1.2 Attending Nephrologist (AN)

**Qualifications**
- PRC-licensed, completed nephrology training in a PSN-accredited/recognized institution.
- In practice locally, accessible to the HDC.
- Applies at the HDC for affiliation with: Curriculum Vitae, updated PHIC accreditation, updated
  PRC license, and **PSN/Local Chapter Endorsement for Attending Nephrologist**.

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Informs ESKD patients of all options (documented informed consent, preferably 2 witnesses).
- Performs complete clinical assessment before initiating hemodialysis.
- Coordinates vascular access with a vascular/trained general surgeon.
- Provides initial and maintenance dialysis prescriptions; medical abstract & medication list.
- **≥1 consultation per month** with each patient (face-to-face or telemedicine).
- Active management with the POD and nurse on duty; coordinates emergencies.
- Long-term medical planning; inter-HDC coordination & endorsement forms on transfer/travel.
- Complies with PhilHealth/PCSO/DSWD documentary requirements.
- Accepts **no gift or rebate** from the HDC.

### 1.3 Physician on Duty (POD)

**Qualifications**
- PRC-licensed physician attending to the acute care of HD patients during sessions, under the
  supervision of the AN and DCH, in order of priority: board-certified/eligible in Nephrology;
  board-certified/eligible in IM/Pediatrics with ≥3 months in a DOH-licensed HDC; nephrology
  fellows-in-training; qualifying IM/senior residents (≥3 months); private-hospital residents
  (≥6 months); Family Medicine/GP (≥6 months).
- Updated **ACLS** certificate (DOH-accredited provider).
- Updated certificate from a **PSN post-grad course for POD**.
- **Ratio to HD stations ideally 1:15, maximum 1:20.**

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Provides acute care during the duty tour per DOH rules and company policy.
- Communicates/updates the AN on acute problems and emergencies (no outside referral without AN
  or DCH knowledge); carries out AN orders; documents progress notes.
- Refers/endorses patients needing hospital admission.
- **Physically present during HDC hours of operation.**
- Evaluates all patients on duty, reviews prior records/orders, interprets new labs with the AN,
  and applies immediate measures with the AN and HDC nurse.

### 1.4 Dialysis Head Nurse

**Qualifications**
- PRC-licensed nurse (R.A. 9173, *Philippine Nursing Act of 2002*).
- **Certificate of training in nursing care of renal dialysis patients** — structured program,
  **3 months or more** (topics: renal function/labs/nutrition/psychological changes; HD
  principles/prescription/pre-&-post assessment/complications/vascular access; disaster
  preparedness & emergencies; team roles & professionalism).
- Updated **BLS/ACLS** (DOH-accredited provider).
- **At least 2 years' experience as a dialysis nurse.**

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Assesses, plans, organizes and analyzes systems for proper nursing service; reviews care
  delivery and outcomes.
- Conducts regular bedside teaching/conferences for medical staff.
- Assesses staffing patterns and assignments; ensures relievers for emergency absences.
- Ensures strict infection control; initiates/schedules/attends **CQI meetings** with the DCH;
  creates solutions to unit problems with the DCH.

---

### 1.5 Dialysis Nurse

**Qualifications**
- PRC-licensed nurse (R.A. 9173).
- **Certificate of training in nursing care of renal dialysis patients** — structured program,
  **3 months or more** (same topics as Head Nurse).
- Updated **BLS and ACLS** (DOH-accredited provider).
- **Ratio to HD patients not more than 1:4 per shift** (may increase to **1:6** with a Nursing
  Assistant present).

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Orients each patient on HDC policies, rights, privileges and the role of their AN.
- Provides direct care per DOH rules and HDC policy; prepares all materials before each session.
- Carries out doctors' orders; assesses each patient before/during/after treatment.
- Updates the AN and POD on problems; initiates nursing interventions; documents data.
- Monitors all machine settings/parameters and addresses alarms.
- Prepares dialysis access to standard; updates medical records (progress notes, meds,
  interventions, new prescriptions).
- Administers medications as prescribed with monitoring; performs **CPR** with POD/AN on arrest.
- Inventories e-cart medicines and supplies each shift; provides health education; ensures signed,
  witnessed informed consent.

---

### 1.6 Dialysis Nurse Assistant

**Qualifications** (any of)
- B.S. Nursing graduate (underboard); **or**
- B.S. Nursing undergraduate (completed at least 2nd-year level); **or**
- B.S. Midwifery graduate.

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Assists dialysis nurses in providing HD treatment and care.
- Assists patients at admission/discharge (vital signs, pre- and post-weight); reports
  irregularities to the dialysis nurse.
- Assists in cleaning/disinfecting machines and chairs, and in preparing machines pre-treatment.
- Monitors and records vital signs during/after the procedure.
- Runs errands for medicines, dialyzers, supplies, blood/blood products.
- Helps secure patient documents (referral letters, medical abstracts, certificates).

---

### 1.7 Dialysis Technician

**Qualifications**
- **At least a high school graduate.**
- **Certificate of training as a dialysis technician** — structured program, **3 months or more**
  (topics: renal function & HD principles; HD machine structure/function, dialyzer reprocessing,
  eco priming; dialysis water treatment, concentrates & solutions; infection control, waste
  management, disaster preparedness & emergencies).

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Rinses/primes dialyzers; verifies each dialyzer belongs to the correctly identified patient.
- Uses only reused dialyzers that pass the performance test; disposes of failed ones properly.
- Prepares dialysis baths (right formula, proper calibration/settings); rinses & disinfects
  machines before and after each procedure; prepares/calibrates reprocessing machines daily.
- Labels/collects/stores used dialyzers to prevent spillage/contamination; disposes of hazardous
  and non-hazardous waste properly.
- Performs dialyzer reprocessing per **AAMI standards**; records reuse numbers; stores
  reprocessed dialyzers systematically.
- Performs preventive maintenance & records machine hours/repairs/problems.
- Conducts regular disinfection of the unit, HD machines, equipment, reprocessing machines, **RO**
  and water distribution systems; ensures daily RO backwashing/maintenance for safe water.
- Conducts **monthly bacteriology** and **bi-annual chemical analysis** of treated water at
  DOH-accredited labs.
- Assists the HD team in emergencies and performs **BLS** on arrest; observes company policies.

## 2. NON-MEDICAL PERSONNEL

### 2.1 Unit Operations Manager (UOM) / Unit Manager in Hospital (UMH)
*Separate position for non-hospital HDCs with >15 stations; may be concurrent for <15 stations;
Unit Manager or equivalent for hospital-based HDCs.*

**Qualifications**
- **≥2 years** of proven success in operations management or as head of a unit/department
  handling ≥5 employees.
- Leadership, communication and literary skills; understands and implements HDC ordinances/policies.
- Strong budget development/oversight; delegation with organizational control and customer service;
  conflict management and business negotiation.
- **Master's degree** in hospital or business administration (or equivalent) **preferred**; **or**
  a bachelor's degree in operations management, business administration, or a medically related
  four-year course.

**Responsibilities** (Administrative / Clinic Management / Financial)
- Ensures DOH/PHIC/PSN/LGU/payor laws & memos are implemented; monitors day-to-day operations;
  ensures third-party forms are truthfully filled.
- Ensures availability, cost-effectiveness and timeliness of FDA-approved supplies/equipment
  (approved by the DCH); implements administrative policies; **no gifts/rebates**; keeps the unit
  open to other nephrologists' patients.
- Supervises **QAPI** review, service-quality systems, and preventive maintenance/repairs/
  disinfection of HD machines, RO and water distribution systems (with the DCH).
- Ensures HDC financial viability; oversees financial operations/accounts payable; ensures timely
  and correct professional fees/salaries/benefits (PHIC/DOLE); provides the DCH/AN a **monthly
  summary** of collections; ensures PFs paid within **30 days** per PhilHealth.

---

### 2.2 Medical Records Officer & Custodian
*A designated dialysis staff member.*

**Qualifications**
- Training in **ICD-10**, basic records management, and hospital health information management as
  required by DOH.

**Duties & Responsibilities** (not limited to)
- Ensures proper ICD-10 input to each patient's chart/file; custodian/safekeeper of records.
- Functions as Registry coordinator; ensures completeness of records/forms for DOH submission.
- Implements the Hospital/HDC medical-records manual and accepted record-keeping standards.

---

### 2.3 Administrative Staff
*Under the supervision of the UOM/UMH — to ensure efficient non-medical work. The PSN guideline
lists these roles (no per-role qualification detail is specified beyond the list):*

- Accounting Clerk
- PHIC Officer / Clerk
- **Cashier**
- Security Personnel
- **Housekeeping Staff** *(maps to St. John Biocare's "Utility Aide")*
- **Maintenance Staff** *(also relevant to "Utility Aide")*
- Secretary / Clerk / Liaison Officer
- Supply / Procurement Clerk
- Pollution Control Officer
- Safety Officer 2

## Staffing ratios (quick reference)
| Role | PSN ratio |
|---|---|
| Physician on Duty (POD) | 1 : 15 stations (max 1 : 20) |
| Dialysis Nurse | 1 : 4 patients / shift (1 : 6 with a Nursing Assistant) |
| Dialysis Clinical Head | max 3 HDCs (up to 5 in nephrologist-scarce / GIDA areas) |

## Notes for St. John Biocare Careers postings
- The PSN list above is the **regulatory baseline**. Existing site postings (Cashier, Utility
  Aide) map to **Administrative Staff → Cashier / Housekeeping / Maintenance** — PSN sets no
  extra credential bar for these, so their qualifications are set by St. John Biocare.
- Any St. John Biocare addition (e.g. **"at least a high school graduate," "no derogatory work
  record"**) is a house requirement layered on top of the PSN baseline — keep it clearly ours,
  not attributed to PSN.
- For clinical roles (Dialysis Nurse, Head Nurse, Nurse Assistant, Dialysis Technician, POD),
  copy the PSN qualifications verbatim so postings are DOH/PSN-compliant.
